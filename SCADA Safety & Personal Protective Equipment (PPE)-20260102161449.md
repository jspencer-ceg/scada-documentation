## **Additional PPE Recommendations**
These items are not absolutely required to be on site, but are required for specific tasks (not normally included in SCADA scope of work). Additionally some individual projects may require these items for all personnel.
*   Safety glasses / Safety rated prescription glasses with side shields - required if you will be doing any cutting, drilling, grinding operations.
*   Work Gloves - required if you will be doing any material handling
*   Flame Retardant (FR) clothing - CAT 2 (8 cal /cm^2 ).

## **Safety Training Requirements**
In order to go on site without an escort, you must have the following training completed. You may still go on site without the training listed below, but must be escorted on site by a trained person.
*   OHSA 10-Hour construction safety course - [https://www.clicksafety.com/osha-10-construction](https://www.clicksafety.com/osha-10-construction)
*   First Aid and CPR training

## **Prohibited Activities**
SCADA personnel are not permitted to perform the following work activities under any circumstances.
*   Entry into any confined space, trench or excavation
*   Operation of any heavy machinery
*   Work at heights greater than 6' above ground level
*   Live electrical work on any circuit energized with greater than 50V DC or AC
*   Hot work (e.g. cutting, welding)
*   Any work in hazardous environments (explosive atmosphere, toxic substances, etc)

## **Site Visit Procedure**
*   Before going on site, check with the site manager for any site specific training, hazards, and PPE requirements.
*   Attend any site training or daily safety brief as required by specific sites
*   Sign on to any Job Hazard Analysis / Task planning / Work permit as required by site procedures
*   Make sure you have a lightning tracker app on your phone.
*   Know the site 911 address, evacuation routes and muster points, location of the nearest hospital, and how to use your phone to determine your exact position in GPS coordinates (latitude / longitude)
*   If you will be working alone, make sure that another person is aware of your location and check in and out with that person at the start and end of your work.
